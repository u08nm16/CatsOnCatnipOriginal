Life of Pi

After the sinking of a cargo ship, a solitary lifeboat remains bobbing on the wild blue Pacific. The only survivors from the wreck are a sixteen-year-old boy named Pi, a hyena, a wounded zebra, an orangutan and a 450-pound royal bengal tiger. The days pass slowly and the lifeboat's passengers coexist warily. During a bout of temporary blindness brought on by dehydration, Pi has a run-in with another blind castaway...

Fried Green Tomatoes

Evelyn, who's in the sad slump of middle age, and gray-headed Mrs. Threadgoode, who's telling her life story. Her tale includes two more women--the irrepressibly daredevilish tomboy Idgie and her friend Ruth--who back in the thirties ran a little place in Whistle Stop, Alabama. A book offering good coffee, southern barbecue, and all kinds of love and laughter--even an occasional murder.
